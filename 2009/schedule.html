Commonsense-2009 Schedule

Commonsense-2009, the Ninth International Symposium on Logical Formalizations of Commonsense Reasoning, will be held from June 1-3 at the Fields Institute, 222 College Street, on the campus of the University of Toronto, Toronto, Canada.

    MONDAY, JUNE 1, 2009

8:30 - 9:00: Morning Coffee and Checking-in of Registrants

9:00 - 9:15: Welcome (Symposium Chairs and Director/Deputy Director of the Fields Institute)

9:15- 10:45: PAPER SESSION I:
9:15- 9:45: Stavros Vassos, Sebastian Sardina, and Hector Levesque: "Progressing Basic Action Theories with Non-Local Effect Actions"
9:45-10:15: Gerhard Lakemeyer and Hector Levesque: "A Semantical Account of Progression in the Presence of Defaults"
10:15-10:45: Hannes Strass and Michael Thielscher: "Defaults in Action: Nonmonotonic Reasoning about States in Action Calculi"

10:45-11:15: Coffee Break

11:15 - 12:15: INVITED TALK

Tony Cohn, University of Leeds: "Acquiring Commonsense Knowledge from Perceptual Observation"

12:15 - 2:00: Lunch

2:00 - 3:30: PAPER SESSION II:

2:00 - 2:30: Ivan Varzinczak: "How Do I Revise my Agent's Action Theory?"
2:30 - 3:00: Salem Benferhat, Didier Dubois, Henri Prade, and Mary-Anne Williams: "A General Framework for Revising Belief Bases Using Qualitative Jefferey's Rule"
3:00 - 3:30: Richard Booth, Thomas Meyer, and Ivan Varzinczak: "Next Steps in Propositional Horn Contraction"

3:30-4:00: Coffee Break

4:00-5:00: PAPER SESSION III:

4:00-4:30: Shirin Sohrabi, Jorge Baier, and Sheila McIlraith: "HTN Planning with Preferences"
4:30-5:00: Jaesik Choi and Eyal Amir: "Combined Planning and Motion Planning"

6:30-8:30: Symposium Banquet (included in registration)

10:30-11:00 Coffee Break

11:00-12:00: INVITED TALK

Ernie Davis, New York University "Commonsense Reasoning About Chemistry Experiments: Ontologies and Representations"
